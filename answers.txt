C(7, 2)= (7×6) /2 = 21 
so 21 tests.
If we used p<0.05 for each test,
the probability of having at least one false positive is:

1 - (1 - 0.05)^21 = 1 - 0.95^21 = 0.65943837371   [source: https://www.google.com/search?q=1+-+0.95%5E21&rlz=1C1YTUH_enCA1189CA1189&oq=1+-+0.95%5E21&gs_lcrp=EgZjaHJvbWUyBggAEEUYOTIGCAEQBhhA0gEHOTcxajBqN6gCALACAA&sourceid=chrome&ie=UTF-8 ]

so about 65.9%=66%

So the effective p-value of running all 21 t-tests at 0.05 is almost 0.66 
so you have almost a two in three chance of drawing at least one false here.
